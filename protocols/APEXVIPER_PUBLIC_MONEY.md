## 2. THE 70% THRESHOLD
**The Law:** When >70% of public money is on one side, consider the opposite.

**Why It Works:**
- Books shade lines to attract balanced action
- Sharp money often opposes heavy public sides
- Market inefficiency creates value on the other side

**Reverse Line Movement (RLM):**
- **Definition:** Line moves AGAINST the heavily bet side
- **Signal:** Sharps loading opposite position
- **Action:** Follow the line movement, not the public %

**Example:**
```
Scenario: 75% of bets on Cowboys -7
Line Movement: Cowboys -7 â†’ Cowboys -6.5

Analysis: Line dropped DESPITE heavy Cowboys action
Signal: Sharp money on opponent
Action: Consider opponent +6.5
```

## 3. APPLICATION STRATEGY

### When to Fade Public
- [ ] Public % exceeds 70% on one side
- [ ] Line movement opposes public position (RLM)
- [ ] Tax team involved (inflated lines expected)
- [ ] Prime time game (casual bettor overload)
- [ ] Narrative-driven spot (revenge, streak, etc.)

### When NOT to Fade Public
- [ ] Public aligns with sharp indicators
- [ ] Line movement confirms public position
- [ ] Fundamental edge exists (injury, weather)
- [ ] Public % is moderate (50-65%)

## 5. NHL-SPECIFIC PUBLIC PATTERNS

### Overbet Situations
- Home favorites on national TV
- Teams on winning streaks (3+)
- Star player returns from injury
- Playoff positioning games (late season)

### Underbet Situations (Value Targets)
- Road underdogs against tax teams
- Back-to-back scheduling (public assumes fade)
- Teams coming off bad loss (public overreacts)
- Non-glamour teams with solid metrics
