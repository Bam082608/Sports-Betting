## 1. THE FOUNDATION
The mental game is the primary differentiator between a gambler and a professional bettor. Strategies and models are tools; the mind is the operator.

## 2. CORE PHILOSOPHIES

### A. Variance Acceptance
- **The Law of Large Numbers:** In the short term, anything can happen. In the long term, the edge prevails.
- **The "One Away" Test:** How you react to a "bad beat" defines your career.
    - **Amateur:** Tilts, chases, blames.
    - **Pro:** Laughs, analyzes, moves on.

### B. The Discipline of the Fold
- The most profitable trade is often the one you *don't* take.
- **"No Bet" is a position.**
- Protecting capital is more important than potential profit.

### C. Detachment
- Treat money as "units" or "ammo".
- Do not attach emotional value to the money (e.g., "That was my rent money").
- If the money means too much, you are over-leveraged.

## 3. PRACTICAL TECHNIQUES

### The Circuit Breaker
- **Trigger:** 3 consecutive losses or feeling "heated".
- **Action:** Physical walk away. 15 minutes minimum. No screen time.

### The "Sim" Mindset
- View the betting session as a strategy game or simulation.
- Outcomes are data points, not personal attacks.

### The Journal
- Documenting feelings and reactions is as important as documenting bets.
- "Why did I make that bet? Was it FOMO? Was it boredom?"
