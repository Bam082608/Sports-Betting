## 3. THE PRIME TIME LAW
**The Trend:** National spotlight games produce different patterns.  
**The Why:**
- Extra preparation time (10+ days for some matchups)
- Coaching adjustments more pronounced
- Public money inflates favorites

**Prime Time Situations:**
- Thursday Night Football
- Sunday Night Football
- Monday Night Football
- International Games

**Action Rule:**
- **FADE:** Heavy public favorites (>70% betting)
- **LEAN:** Divisional underdogs in prime time
- **TARGET:** Experienced QBs over young QBs (nerves factor)

---

## 4. THE DIVISIONAL LAW
**The Trend:** Divisional games are tighter, more competitive.  
**The Why:**
- Teams know each other intimately
- Coaching familiarity reduces blowouts
- Revenge narratives more potent

**Action Rule:**
- **LEAN:** Divisional underdogs to cover
- **FADE:** Large spreads (-7.5 or more) in division games
- **TARGET:** Second meeting of the season (revenge factor amplified)

---

## 5. THE REVENGE LAW
**The Trend:** Teams with embarrassing losses often rebound strongly.  
**The Triggers:**
- Blowout loss (20+ points) in recent matchup
- Season sweep scenarios
- Playoff elimination memories

**Evidence:** Bills revenge games following playoff losses show elevated performance metrics.

**Action Rule:**
- **IDENTIFY:** Teams with clear revenge narrative
- **TARGET:** Revenge team in competitive spots
- **AVOID:** Fading revenge teams as large underdogs
