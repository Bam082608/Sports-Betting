## ‚ö†Ô∏è CRITICAL OPERATIONAL MANDATES (Hard-Coded in Both Nodes)

### **1. THE NHL MANDATE (Absolute Law)**

**RULE:** PERMANENT BAN on Full Game Totals (Over/Under)

**Rationale:**
- Full game totals are high-variance coin flips
- End-game chaos (empty nets) creates uncontrollable variance
- Cannot isolate specific advantages
- Historical failure pattern documented (Four Horsemen event 12/11/25)

**Execution:**
- ALL NHL betting must use **Team Totals** exclusively
- Example: "Rangers Team Total Over 3.5" ‚úÖ
- Example: "Game Total Over 6.5" ‚ùå **REJECTED**
- Both nodes must reject/correct any game total mention

**Enforcement Mechanism:**
- Encyclopedia: Reject in initial analysis
- Claude: Reject in final audit
- If game total appears ‚Üí Automatic pivot to team totals

---

### **2. THE SPIDER WEB STRATEGY**

**RULE:** Every play must have explicit classification

**ANCHOR:**
- **Definition:** Foundational, high-confidence play based on structural mismatch
- **Examples:**
  - Rest vs Fatigue advantage
  - GSAx Delta (goalie quality gap)
  - Team DNA alignment
  - Power Play vs Penalty Kill mismatch
- **Allocation:** Primary bet size ($2-5)
- **Requirement:** Must stand alone logically
- **Independence:** Not dependent on other bets

**INDEPENDENT WEB:**
- **Definition:** Situational play with lower confidence, often correlated to Anchor
- **Examples:**
  - Period-specific props
  - Player props aligned with game script
  - Hedge positions
  - Value spots with higher risk
- **Allocation:** Secondary bet size ($0.75-2)
- **Requirement:** Must have independent justification
- **Risk Profile:** Higher variance tolerated

**Prohibited:**
- Unclassified plays ("I just like it")
- Bets without structural justification
- Emotional/narrative-driven recommendations
- Correlation traps (multiple bets on same outcome)

**Enforcement:**
- Encyclopedia: Tag each play as Anchor/Web in output
- Claude: Verify classification in final audit
- No play approved without explicit category

---

### **3. THE RAT POISON PROTOCOL**

**RULE:** Active scanning for trap lines

**Definition:** "Rat Poison" = Lines that look too good to be true

**Red Flags:**
1. **Mismatch Pricing**
   - Top Tier vs Bottom Tier with short price (-120 to -140)
   - Obvious advantage with suspiciously good odds

2. **Public Hammer Games**
   - High betting volume on one side
   - Line doesn't move despite action
   - Implies sharp money on other side

3. **Narrative Traps**
   - Revenge games
   - "Must-win" situations
   - Media hype inflating perceived edge

4. **Historical Patterns**
   - Team has burned public repeatedly
   - Classic letdown spot
   - Schedule trap

**Execution Protocol:**
1. **Encyclopedia:** Preliminary sweep, flag suspicious lines
2. **Claude:** Secondary verification, demand explanation
3. **Decision Tree:**
   - Can we justify the odds? ‚Üí PROCEED
   - Is justification weak? ‚Üí INVESTIGATE
   - Still unclear? ‚Üí **SKIP** (capital preservation priority)

**Response Framework:**
```
IF line_looks_easy:
    ASK: "Why is this line so good?"
    IF answer_is_solid:
        PROCEED (but reduce stake)
    ELSE:
        SKIP (preserve capital)
```

**Philosophy:** Better to miss +EV than lose to trap
